# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Former dbB clan members, friends, and retro Quake players who arrive with nostalgia rather than a task to complete. Visitors are expected to spend a brief, quiet moment with the mark and then leave; the page does not need to explain, convert, or route them anywhere.

## Product Purpose

This site is a static memorial for dbB, an inactive Austrian/German gaming clan founded in the late 1990s with its center of gravity in Quake games. Its purpose is to preserve the clan's emblem as a digital tombstone: one image, one atmosphere, no interface clutter.

Success means the viewer immediately understands they are looking at a preserved relic, not a modern landing page, fan portal, or recruitment site.

## Brand Personality

Solemn, industrial, and archival. The tone should feel like a late-night visit to an abandoned gothic Quake map where the clan mark survives as weathered stone.

## Anti-references

Do not make this a clan-history page, portfolio, game portal, recruitment page, or marketing site. Avoid visible navigation, calls to action, explanatory copy, card layouts, glass effects, startup gradients, purple fog, playful horror props, and generic esports/neon styling. The design should not feel like a modern SaaS dark mode or a Halloween poster.

## Design Principles

1. Let the image be the memorial.
2. Treat darkness as material, not decoration.
3. Preserve late-1990s Quake weight without turning it into cosplay.
4. Hide interface mechanics; expose only atmosphere.
5. Respect absence: no engagement patterns, no unnecessary motion, no visual noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA where applicable. The visible page can remain image-only, but semantic HTML, meaningful alt text, screen-reader-only context, metadata, reduced-motion handling, and coarse-pointer fallbacks are required. Motion must never gate access to the image, and the custom cursor must fall back to native cursor behavior on touch devices or reduced-motion preferences.
